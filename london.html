Background

  • During my Study Abroad trip to Paris in Summer 2010, we were able/encouraged to fly to other cities in Europe on the weekends since the price of transportation to other European cities is usually very reasonable.
  • Two of the five roommates that I had in Paris, Daria and Maddy, and I decided to take the Eurostar Train from Paris to London - it goes underneath the channel!

    • Photo caption: A Picture Explaining going under the Channel between France and England

  • I fell asleep when we went under the channel. Maddy said that it just feels like one really dark tunnel and wasn't anything too spectacular. We arrived in St. Pancras Station (the international counterpart to Kings Cross Station) at 10:30pm Paris time (9:30pm London time). The station was very nice, modern, and clean. They have a towering sculpture of a man and a woman kissing to greet you as you get off the train and enter the station. The marketing/advertisements throughout the station includes a picture of that sculpture with "Meet Me at St. Pancras!" as the catchphrase. Kings Cross and St. Pancras connect and feel more like an airport due to sheer size rather than a train station.

    • Photo caption: Meet Me at St. Pancras Statue - a Couple Embracing

  • The walk from Kings Cross to our hostel in Russel Square took only about 20 minutes once we got our bearings. Compared to Paris, the streets were very quiet and not as well-lit, but they were much much cleaner. Our hostel looked like a nice place from the outside, but very crowded on the inside. Our twelve bed hostel room was like a very long dorm room lined with bunk beds on each wall with a small aisle to walk down the middle. Our hostel overall was very large - at one point I overheard the receptionist say that there were 94 beds free for Saturday evening. This hostel made me truly appreciate our small apartment in Paris that I shared with only 4 other ladies!

London - Day One

  • After a quick (free) breakfast at the hostel, we headed out towards Buckingham Palace. Along the way, we passed Trafalgar Square that was all set up to celebrate the first day of the 2010 World Cup in South Africa! Even though England wasn't playing in the first day of the World Cup, there was still so much excitement!

    • Photo caption: Trafalgar Square World Cup 2010 Celebration

    Photo caption: Trafalgar Square World Cup 2010 Celebration

    Photo caption: Trafalgar Square World Cup 2010 Celebration

    Photo caption: Trafalgar Square World Cup 2010 Celebration

  • Dollymount Beach looked like a dried up pier at first because of the low tide. We kept walking and discovered the most powdery soft sand and a great view of the ocean! There were so many dogs at the beach - dogs in Europe seem so well-behaved! Daria and I were so excited to find small fragments of shells until we found the endless amounts of beautiful whole shells. Even though the water was cold and the tide was low, we enjoyed the view. Once we had gathered as many shells as we could carry, we headed back towards the bus stop. We quizzically watched the people digging in the mud on our way back, wondering what they could be searching for.

    • Photo caption: Dollymount Beach in Dublin, Ireland

  • After we dropped our shells off at the hostel, we headed out again towards Dame Street to explore and see Trinity College, one of the oldest and best colleges in Dublin.

    • Photo caption: Trinity College in Dublin, Ireland

  • Dame Street was lined with gorgeous brick buildings and also rainbow flags! This year (2010) was to be Dublin's very first Pride Parade! We were so disappointed that we would not be here for the festivities (we ended up have a fantastic time at Paris' Pride Parade). Daria and I found a couple interesting indoor markets and a Euro2 (similar to a Dollar Store).

    • Photo caption: Dame Street in Dublin, Ireland

  • Our next stop was Christ Church. We were unable to go inside because of a wedding but I couldn't help but think about how beautiful it would be to get married there.

    • Photo caption: Christ Church Cathedral in Dublin, Ireland

    Photo caption: Christ Church Cathedral in Dublin, Ireland

  • We also passed Saint Auddens Polish Church. They had signs out front to help raise money for the flood victims in Poland. Daria explained to me that her father's farm in Poland flooded because they opened a dam there.

    • Photo caption: Saint Auddens Polish Church in Dublin, Ireland

  • We walked back towards Bridge Street and passed the Brazen Head Bar, the oldest bar in Dublin! It was very cute, but much smaller than we had expected.

    • Photo caption: Brazen Head Pub in Dublin, Ireland

  • We crossed River Liffey and walked past St. Paul's Church as well as the Four Courts (home of the Supreme Court, the High Court, and the Dublin Circuit Court that has an enormous rotunda).

    • Photo caption: Four Courts in Dublin, Ireland

  • For dinner, Daria and I decided to try a traditional Irish Place called Gallagher's Boxty House, recommended by our hostel. The chicken boxty that I had was delicious - a traditional Irish potato pancake filled with chicken. For dessert, we had homemade ice cream, a yummy treat that was a welcome change from all of the gelato that we had in Paris.

    • Photo caption: Homemade Ice Cream at Gallagher's Boxty House in Dublin, Ireland

  • After dinner, we headed to O'Connell Street to see the famous Dublin Post Office and the Spire of Dublin. The Spire looks like an enormous needle that is remarkably easy to spot from a distance. Because we had seen so many Georgian Doors on the way to the beach and on postcards, we especially wanted to see the North Georgian area - promised to have a high concentration of Georgian Doors. The doors are so colourful - they just feel so friendly and inviting!

  • As we walked along the river to the Custom House, we ran into a very drunk Irish man. He wanted to tell us about the statue on top of the Custom House and how it is the last British statue in Dublin. He belligerently scolded us for not knowing more of Ireland's history and we then excused ourselves from the conversation.

    • Photo caption: Custom House in Dublin, Ireland

    Photo caption: Statue on top of Custom House in Dublin, Ireland

  • The Samuel Beckett Bridge is one of my favorite bridges in the world (it goes head to head with the Golden Gate Bridge). It was designed by Santiago Calatrava to be in the shape of the Dublin Harp. Dublin, like Paris, stays fairly light outside until about 10 or 11pm so by the time we had gotten to this bridge, it was beginning to get dark. We encountered other drunk and slightly creepy men so we made our way quickly back to the hostel.

Dublin - Day Two

  • It was a gorgeous day outside and we felt so lucky to be in Dublin! After breakfast, we walked back to the Dublin Post Office to mail postcards and also to see what it was like during the day. We then tried to catch a bus to Saint Patrick's Cathedral but found out that they were not running in that direction today. We decided to walk since it was not that far and on our way we got to see Dublin Castle. It wasn't the typical castle that I picture, but obviously very nice. We then figured out that the buses were not running in the area because they were setting up for the Halford's Tour Bike Race!

    • Photo caption: Dublin Castle in Dublin, Ireland

    Photo caption: Dublin Castle in Dublin, Ireland

  • Saint Patrick's Cathedral was jaw-dropping! We had seen beautiful cathedrals in Paris - Notre Dame, Saint Chapelle, etc but this one felt much older and more regal in my humble opinion.

    • Photo caption: Saint Patrick's Cathedral in Dublin, Ireland

    Photo caption: Saint Patrick's Cathedral in Dublin, Ireland

    Photo caption: Saint Patrick's Cathedral in Dublin, Ireland

    Photo caption: Saint Patrick's Cathedral in Dublin, Ireland

  • For lunch we stopped for smoothies near Christ Church as well as a delicious sandwich shop called Cafe Noto. We sat on the back steps outside Christ Church to eat and had a wonderful time people watching.
  • Once we had rested our legs enough (and sufficiently eavesdropped on the interesting lives of local Dublin-ers) we headed towards the Guinness Storehouse/Brewery. This was my first brewery tour of all time and I think that it somewhat ruined brewery tours for me forever because who could top possibly top this?! It was so much better than I had expected - the tour buildings were so modern and it all takes place inside the world's largest pint glass. We did not see the beer being made, but we watched many videos and learned about the history as well as the four simple ingredients that go into Guinness Beer.

    • Photo caption: Guinness Brewery in Dublin, Ireland

    Photo caption: Guinness Brewery in Dublin, Ireland

  • During the tour, we were able to sample some Guinness. At the end of the tour, at the top of the brewery, you have two options: either drink a pint of Guinness while looking at a gorgeous panoramic view of the city OR learn how to pour your own perfect pint of Guinness and see a slightly less gorgeous view of Dublin while you drink it. Daria and I obviously chose the latter option. The young man who taught us how to pour our pints had the greatest accent and even said "rubbish". I found out that Guinness tastes much better in Ireland that in the States. The Guinness Brewery tour was one of my favorite parts of Europe and I strongly recommend it.

    • Photo caption: Pour your own Perfect Pint at the Guinness Brewery in Dublin, Ireland

    Photo caption: Pour your own Perfect Pint at the Guinness Brewery in Dublin, Ireland

Dublin - Day Three

  • Once we had packed our bags and stored them with the front desk of the hostel, we decided to go to Grafton Street. Before going to the mall and stores, we stopped by Stephen's Green Park, one of the largest parks in Dublin. It includes many statues and even a garden for the blind!

    • Photo caption: Stephen's Green Park in Dublin, Ireland

    Photo caption: Stephen's Green Park in Dublin, Ireland

  • We then stopped by the mall named for the park. It actually feels like you are in a large green house because of the atrium style glass ceiling.

    • Photo caption: Grafton Street Mall in Dublin, Ireland

  • Next, we decided to go back to Dame Street to explore the indoor markets further to try to find presents for our roommates back in Paris. We stopped for lunch on Dame Street and I had the most delicious mango quesadillas that I would love to be able to replicate at home sometime!
  • The bus to the airport from the hostel only took 20 minutes rather than the hour we had expected so we had plenty of time to journal and write more postcards. Our trip back to the apartment in Paris from the airport was an interesting journey. The train that would take us to the city from the airport was closed so we were routed onto buses without much understanding of where they would take us. Luckily, we eventually made it back to the apartment safely and we were very excited to see our lovely roommates and hear about their adventures. Instead of going to sleep right away, I wrote my paper that was due the next day for our Political Science class.

Summary

Dublin ended up being my favorite city from my trip to Europe. I absolutely loved the hospitality and friendliness. All of the sites were accessible by foot and in my opinion it was the cleanest of the cities we had visited. I feel so blessed to have had the opportunity to have visited their wonderful city and experience some of their culture. Even though they were not in the World Cup Finals that year, they still had so much spirit and their restaurants looked so festive with all of the decorations. The idea behind the Claddagh: love, loyalty, and friendship is the perfect way to describe Ireland. I hope that I am lucky enough to return someday soon. We only spent a few days in Dublin, but I feel like we saw a vast majority of their popular tourist attractions. I would especially love to go back to see more of their country-side and non-urban areas.
