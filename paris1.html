Background

The summer between my junior and senior year of college, I studied abroad for a month in Paris, France!

I had four roommates: Daria, Lisa, Maddy, and Urba. Our classmates were: Jad, Neil, Khalid, Xiaochen, Xiaofeng, and Wenkan. Although we were all students at the Illinois Institute of Technology, I had only met Urba before the planning for this trip began. Together in Paris we all took a political science class about Global Economies taught by Professor Laura Hosman and a psychology class about Impressionist painters with Professor Michael Young.

I've split my journals into weeks in Paris so that they are more manageable and so that you don't get a headache reading one long page about a month in Paris. I also have separate journals about weekend trips to Dublin and London.

Paris - Day 1

Urba and I met at the Charles de Gaulle (CDG) airport and took the Super Shuttle together to our apartment (in the second arrondissement). I highly recommend Super Shuttle - they were very nice and less stressful than taking the Metro (underground public transportation system and the cheapest option) and less expensive than taking a taxi (the most convenient option). One of our other roommates took a taxi to our apartment and it cost 54 euros compared to the 25 euros that we paid for the Super Shuttle.

Once we arrived at our apartment, we did not know the code to get into the building and didn't have our keys yet. We had no way of getting in contact with our other roommates, but we were able to sneak in behind the postal lady. Once inside, we weren't sure which section of the building we were in. We decided to explore the building and shoved all of our suitcases into a tiny elevator (probably uncomfortable for two people to even stand in) and sat on top of them to ride to the second floor. Once upstairs, we tried ringing doorbells (which were not easy to associate to the correct apartment since they were haphazardly placed on different floors and throughout the hallway). After Urba rang a man's doorbell twice on accident, he kindly answered and told us that only families lived in that section of the building. We then dragged our luggage to the next section of the building and were about to go up the stairs when we saw Maddy and Daria (two of our roommates) enter the apartment building. Relieved, we went to see our apartment building (actually not in the section that were about to try next).

We had rented a very cozy studio apartment with a small lofted area that managed to fit beds for 6 people total (five of us would be sharing the apartment). The bathroom (especially for five girls) was small, but we had an understanding that we really wouldn't be spending much time in the apartment anyway. The building itself was interesting - instead of being completely enclosed, the middle had a large courtyard area so all of the apartments were able to get sunshine and fresh air.

After Urba and I got settled and cleaned up, we all went to explore the second arrondissement near the apartment while waiting for Lisa (our final roommate) to arrive from the airport. A few hours later, we found her casually sitting in a cafe next to our apartment waiting for us (in retrospect that seemed like a much easier way to find us than randomly knocking on apartments in the building - but also not as much fun).

Once Lisa got settled, we went to find our school on Rue Lafayette (Illinois Institute of Technology has studio space in Paris for its architecture students, but other study abroad programs are able to use the space for classes since the architecture students are mostly out on walking tours of the city) that was also in the second arrondissement not too far from our apartment - just a quick ten minute walk.

We then went to Monoprix (a store very similar to Target with a decent grocery section) to buy fixings for dinner. They actually sell almost all the same personal hygiene products so we could have purchased them there instead of bringing them with us from the States. I deeply regretted taking out my jacket at the airport when my suitcase was overweight when I could have taken out shampoo and conditioner! Paris is actually chilly and rainy compared to Chicago in May/June.

I also noticed right away that a lot of the signage in Paris is actually conveniently in both French and English.

After a quick dinner at our apartment, we decided to walk to the Eiffel Tower. It took us about two hours each way (utterly exhausting after a long international flight) but it was totally worth every blister. The Eiffel Tower is gorgeous at night and there are a lot of sites along the way, especially in Central Paris. We saved going to the top for another night since it was getting late and there was a long line (it was Saturday night). It didn't really hit me until seeing the Eiffel Tower that I was in Paris and at least for the summer the world was my oyster. This was a wonderful way to kick-off a beautiful summer in Paris.

We were so disappointed that we would not be here for the festivities (we ended up have a fantastic time at Paris' Pride Parade). Daria and I found a couple interesting indoor markets and a Euro2 (similar to a Dollar Store).

The chicken boxty that I had was delicious - a traditional Irish potato pancake filled with chicken. Because we had seen so many Georgian Doors on the way to the beach and on postcards, we especially wanted to see the North Georgian area - promised to have a high concentration of Georgian Doors. The doors are so colourful - they just feel so friendly and inviting!

He belligerently scolded us for not knowing more of Ireland's history and we then excused ourselves from the conversation.

The Samuel Beckett Bridge is one of my favorite bridges in the world (it goes head to head with the Golden Gate Bridge). It was designed by Santiago Calatrava to be in the shape of the Dublin Harp. Dublin, like Paris, stays fairly light outside until about 10 or 11pm so by the time we had gotten to this bridge, it was beginning to get dark. We encountered other drunk and slightly creepy men so we made our way quickly back to the hostel.

Dublin - Day Two

It was a gorgeous day outside and we felt so lucky to be in Dublin! After breakfast, we walked back to the Dublin Post Office to mail postcards and also to see what it was like during the day. We then tried to catch a bus to Saint Patrick's Cathedral but found out that they were not running in that direction today. We decided to walk since it was not that far and on our way we got to see Dublin Castle. It wasn't the typical castle that I picture, but obviously very nice. We then figured out that the buses were not running in the area because they were setting up for the Halford's Tour Bike Race!

Photo caption: Dublin Castle in Dublin, Ireland

Photo caption: Dublin Castle in Dublin, Ireland

Saint Patrick's Cathedral was jaw-dropping! We had seen beautiful cathedrals in Paris - Notre Dame, Saint Chapelle, etc but this one felt much older and more regal in my humble opinion.

For lunch we stopped for smoothies near Christ Church as well as a delicious sandwich shop called Cafe Noto. We sat on the back steps outside Christ Church to eat and had a wonderful time people watching.

Once we had rested our legs enough (and sufficiently eavesdropped on the interesting lives of local Dublin-ers) we headed towards the Guinness Storehouse/Brewery. This was my first brewery tour of all time and I think that it somewhat ruined brewery tours for me forever because who could top possibly top this?! It was so much better than I had expected - the tour buildings were so modern and it all takes place inside the world's largest pint glass. We did not see the beer being made, but we watched many videos and learned about the history as well as the four simple ingredients that go into Guinness Beer.

Photo caption: Guinness Brewery in Dublin, Ireland

Photo caption: Guinness Brewery in Dublin, Ireland

During the tour, we were able to sample some Guinness. At the end of the tour, at the top of the brewery, you have two options: either drink a pint of Guinness while looking at a gorgeous panoramic view of the city OR learn how to pour your own perfect pint of Guinness and see a slightly less gorgeous view of Dublin while you drink it. Daria and I obviously chose the latter option. The young man who taught us how to pour our pints had the greatest accent and even said "rubbish". I found out that Guinness tastes much better in Ireland that in the States. The Guinness Brewery tour was one of my favorite parts of Europe and I strongly recommend it.

Dublin - Day Three

Once we had packed our bags and stored them with the front desk of the hostel, we decided to go to Grafton Street. Before going to the mall and stores, we stopped by Stephen's Green Park, one of the largest parks in Dublin. It includes many statues and even a garden for the blind!

Photo caption: Stephen's Green Park in Dublin, Ireland

Photo caption: Stephen's Green Park in Dublin, Ireland

We then stopped by the mall named for the park. It actually feels like you are in a large green house because of the atrium style glass ceiling.

Photo caption: Grafton Street Mall in Dublin, Ireland

Next, we decided to go back to Dame Street to explore the indoor markets further to try to find presents for our roommates back in Paris. We stopped for lunch on Dame Street and I had the most delicious mango quesadillas that I would love to be able to replicate at home sometime!

The bus to the airport from the hostel only took 20 minutes rather than the hour we had expected so we had plenty of time to journal and write more postcards. Our trip back to the apartment in Paris from the airport was an interesting journey. The train that would take us to the city from the airport was closed so we were routed onto buses without much understanding of where they would take us. Luckily, we eventually made it back to the apartment safely and we were very excited to see our lovely roommates and hear about their adventures. Instead of going to sleep right away, I wrote my paper that was due the next day for our Political Science class.

Summary

Dublin ended up being my favorite city from my trip to Europe. I absolutely loved the hospitality and friendliness. All of the sites were accessible by foot and in my opinion it was the cleanest of the cities we had visited. I feel so blessed to have had the opportunity to have visited their wonderful city and experience some of their culture. Even though they were not in the World Cup Finals that year, they still had so much spirit and their restaurants looked so festive with all of the decorations. The idea behind the Claddagh: love, loyalty, and friendship is the perfect way to describe Ireland. I hope that I am lucky enough to return someday soon. We only spent a few days in Dublin, but I feel like we saw a vast majority of their popular tourist attractions. I would especially love to go back to see more of their country-side and non-urban areas.
